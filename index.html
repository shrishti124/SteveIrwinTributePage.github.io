A little about Steve Irwin

Steve Irwin inherited a love of large reptiles early on in life and shared his love and passion through his work at the Australia Zoo and his popular TV series The Crocodile Hunter. He was also nicknamed "The Crocodile Hunter" and grew up taking care and studying animals on his parent's wildlife park, and became a well-known figure in popular culture with his trademark khaki shirt and shorts.


Steve Irwin was a loving father and husband who dedicated his life to protecting wildlife and wild places. He accomplished this life mission through his wildlife documentary series The Crocodile Hunter, his world famous Australia Zoo, and his charity Wildlife Warriors. He was best known for his rescue and research work with endangered saltwater crocodiles, the closest living relative of the dinosaurs, now the largest reptiles on earth. Today we continue his mission by working to overturn recent legislation in Queensland which allows crocodile egg harvesting from vulnerable crocs in the wild to support the commercial leather trade. Steve’s legacy will definitely live on.

-- Terri Irwin's thoughts on Steve's legacy

Here's a timeline of Steve Robert Irwin's life:

  • 1962 - Born in Essendon, a suburban area located in Melbourne, Australia.
  • 1970 - Moved to Queensland where he attended school. He also wrestled his first crocodile at age 9.
  • 1991 - First met Terri Raines, an American naturalist from Eugene, Oregon, who was visiting wildlife rehabilitation facilities in Australia and had decided to visit the zoo. They were later engaged.
  • 1992 - Steve took over the management of the Queensland's East Coast Crocodile Management program. He renamed the program as the "Autralia Zoo". The zoo later was later voted as the top tourist destination for Queensland which would attract American tourists to visit Australia.
  • 1996 - Steve and Terry filmed the first episode of The Crocodile Hunter which was during their honeymoon while they were trapping crocodiles. The programming later debuted on Australian TV networks.
  • 1997 - The Crocodile Hunter made its way onto American Television. This became a great success all throughout the United States, the UK, and over 130 other countries. reaching 500 million people.
  • 1998 - Steve and his wife gave birth to their daughter and first child Bindi Irwin.
  • 2001 - Steve appeared in a cameo role in the Eddie Murphy film Dr. Dolittle 2.
  • 2002 - Steve stared in his own film The Crocodile Hunter: Collision Course. Despite its mixed reviews he won an award for Best Family Feature Film for a comedy film at the Young Artist Awards ceremony.
  • 2003 - Steve and his wife gave birth to their second child and son Bob Irwin.
  • 2004 - He was appointed ambassador for The Ghan, a passenger train running from Adelaide to Alice Springs in the central Australian outback.
  • 2005 - He played as a voice actor in the animated film Happy Feet as an elephant seal named Trev.
  • 2006 - Steve Irwin died at age 44 from stingray puncture to his chest. His funeral was later held in the Australia Zoo where he was also buried.
